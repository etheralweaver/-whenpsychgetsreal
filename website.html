üåü Two World-Class Systems

Both Canada and the UK offer excellent pathways to becoming an educational psychologist, each with unique advantages:


üá®üá¶ Canada: Provincial variation allows flexibility in requirements. Some provinces accept Master's degrees for full licensure, making it potentially faster and more accessible. Strong funding opportunities and research focus.


üá¨üáß United Kingdom: More structured pathways with clear specialisation options. Direct government funding for doctoral training. Strong emphasis on school-based practice and multi-disciplinary working.

Canadian Educational Journey Timeline

Bachelor's Degree

4 years

Options: BA or BSc in Psychology

Key Requirements:

  • Minimum GPA 3.5/4.0 or 80%+
  • Research experience (honours thesis recommended)
  • Core courses: Statistics, Research Methods, Developmental Psychology

üí° Tip: BSc often preferred for research-focused programs

Master's Degree

2-3 years

Options: MA, MEd, or MSc

Specialisations:

  • School & Clinical Child Psychology
  • Counselling Psychology
  • Special Education
  • Educational Psychology

Funding: $18,000-$23,000/year typical

Doctoral Degree (Optional/Required)

4-6 years

Options: PhD, PsyD, or EdD

CPA-Accredited Programs:

  • University of Alberta
  • University of Calgary
  • OISE - University of Toronto
  • McGill University
  • UBC
  • Western University

Funding: $28,000+/year typical

Supervised Experience

1-4 years (varies by province)

Requirements vary:

  • Alberta: 1,600 hours (1 year)
  • Nova Scotia: 4 years for Master's holders
  • Quebec: 2,300 hours
  • Must be supervised by registered psychologist

Examinations & Licensure

6-12 months

Required Exams:

  • EPPP Part 1 (Knowledge)
  • EPPP Part 2 (Skills)
  • Provincial Jurisprudence/Ethics Exam
  • Additional exams in some provinces

Result: Licensed Psychologist or Psychological Associate
