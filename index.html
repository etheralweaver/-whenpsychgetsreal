🎓 UK School Psychology Career Roadmap

Your journey from psychology graduate to school mental health professional

🚀 Start Here: Psychology Degree Required

All pathways begin with a BPS-accredited psychology degree or conversion course to achieve Graduate Basis for Chartered Membership (GBC)

🧠
Educational Psychologist

Key Requirements:

  • BPS-accredited psychology degree (2:1 or higher preferred)
  • Minimum 1 year full-time experience with children/young people
  • 3-year Doctorate in Educational Psychology (England/Wales/NI)
  • HCPC registration required
1
Build Experience
Work in education, health, social care, or youth justice settings. Teaching experience highly valuable.
2
Apply for Doctorate
Competitive entry via AEP for England. Wales/NI have separate processes. Government funded via EPFT scheme.
3
Complete Training
Year 1: University-based. Years 2-3: Practice placements + research dissertation.
4
Register & Practice
HCPC registration, BPS membership, then work in local authorities, schools, or private practice.

💰 Salary Expectations:

Newly Qualified: £42,422 - £61,848

Senior/Principal: £52,678 - £86,587

Scotland: £54,438 - £85,170

💝
School Therapy Professionals

Common Entry Routes:

  • Psychology degree + counselling/therapy training
  • Postgraduate diploma in counselling psychology
  • Play therapy or art therapy qualifications
  • BACP or HCPC registration often required
1
Gain Experience
Work as psychological support worker, teaching assistant, or in mental health services.
2
Specialist Training
Complete counselling, play therapy, or other therapeutic training programs.
3
School-Based Practice
Work directly in schools providing individual/group therapy interventions.

💰 Salary Range:

School Counsellor: £25,000 - £35,000

Senior Therapist: £35,000 - £45,000

👩‍🏫
Teaching with Psychology Specialism

Route Options:

  • PGCE/School Direct teacher training
  • Psychology degree provides QTS eligibility
  • SEN specialism highly valuable
  • Can teach psychology A-levels
1
Teacher Training
Complete PGCE, School Direct, or alternative teacher training route.
2
Classroom Experience
Build teaching experience, potentially specializing in SEN or pastoral care.
3
Psychology Leadership
Lead mental health initiatives, become Head of Psychology, or SEN coordinator.

💰 Teaching Salaries:

NQT: £28,000 - £34,500

Experienced: £38,000 - £60,000+

🏥
CAMHS & NHS School Links

Typical Requirements:

  • Psychology degree + relevant experience
  • Mental health training or qualifications
  • Understanding of NHS structures
  • Often requires additional clinical training
1
NHS Experience
Work as healthcare assistant, support worker, or in mental health services.
2
CAMHS Training
Specialized training in child and adolescent mental health approaches.
3
School Liaison
Bridge between schools and mental health services, providing consultation and support.

💰 NHS Bands:

Band 4-5: £23,000 - £31,000

Band 6-7: £32,000 - £45,000

🔬
Educational Psychology Research

Academic Pathway:

  • Strong research background from undergraduate
  • Master's or PhD in educational/developmental psychology
  • Publication and grant writing experience
  • University or research institute positions
1
Research Experience
Research assistant roles, master's research project, or PhD studies.
2
Academic Position
Lecturer, research fellow, or postdoc positions in universities.
3
Applied Research
Collaborate with schools on research projects, inform policy and practice.

💰 Academic Salaries:

Research Assistant: £25,000 - £35,000

Lecturer: £35,000 - £55,000

Senior Academic: £55,000 - £80,000+

💡 Key Success Factors

Experience: All pathways value hands-on experience with children/young people | Networking: Join BPS, AEP, and attend conferences | Continuous Learning: Stay updated with research and best practices
