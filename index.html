ðŸ‡¨ðŸ‡¦ Canadian Educational Journey

Complete pathway from undergraduate studies to professional licensure

Bachelor's Degree

4 years
Requirements: Minimum GPA 3.5/4.0, research experience, core courses in statistics, research methods, and developmental psychology. Honours thesis recommended for competitive programs.

Master's Degree

2-3 years
Specializations: School & Clinical Child Psychology, Counselling Psychology, Special Education, Educational Psychology. Funding: SSHRC CGS-M provides $27,000/year.

Doctoral Degree

4-6 years
CPA-Accredited Programs: University of Alberta, Calgary, OISE-Toronto, McGill, UBC, Western, Manitoba, Saskatchewan, and others. Funding: SSHRC Doctoral Fellowship provides $40,000/year. Required for full licensure in most provinces.

Canadian Success Checklist

Research Provincial Requirements
Identify target province and understand specific licensure requirements
Excel in Undergraduate Studies
Maintain 3.5+ GPA, complete key psychology courses and research methods
Gain Research Experience
Complete honours thesis, work as research assistant, present at conferences
Build Practical Experience
Volunteer or work with children and youth in educational settings
Join Professional Associations
CPA student membership, provincial psychology associations

ðŸ‡¬ðŸ‡§ UK Educational Psychology Path

Structured route from psychology graduate to qualified educational psychologist

Psychology Degree (BPS Accredited)

3 years
Requirements: BPS accredited degree providing Graduate Basis for Chartered Membership (GBC). Minimum 2:2 Honours required for eligibility, though 2:1 preferred for competitive doctoral programs.

Relevant Experience

1-2 years
Essential: Work with children/young people in education, health, or social care. Teaching assistant, support worker, or research assistant roles highly valued.

Doctoral Training Programme

3 years
Funded Training: Department for Education funds programmes through the Educational Psychology Funded Training (EPFT) scheme. Combines academic study with supervised practice placements. Leads to qualification as Educational Psychologist with HCPC registration.
